Heritage Insights

Nyonya tiles, or "Majolica tiles," were imported to the Straits Settlements in the early 20th century. They symbolize the hybrid identity of the Peranakan community, blending Victorian techniques with Chinese symbolism.

HCI Research Methodology

1. Theoretical Framework: This prototype leverages Digital Storytelling and Tangible Interaction concepts to lower the barrier for cultural heritage engagement.


2. User-Centric Design: Inspired by Dr. Marina's 2026 research on No-code authoring tools, I utilized AI-assisted rapid prototyping to iterate user flows, ensuring accessibility for non-technical users.


3. Narrative Engine: The branching logic simulates Heuristic Exploration, allowing users to build a personal connection with artifacts through "Digital Dialogues" rather than passive observation.


4. Future Scope: I plan to integrate Augmented Reality (AR) markers to overlay these digital narratives onto physical Nyonya tiles in a museum setting, enhancing the Presence and Immersion factors.
